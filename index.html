Home

My name is Tom, I am a computer scientist who works on two major areas of research: High-dimensional Long-horizon Planning and Intrinsic Motivation, both of which build on the Options Framework of Reinforcement Learning. "High-dimensional long-horizon planning" refers to tasks where the agent must achieve sequences of goals while tracking many dynamic state-variables (such as hunger or thirst states), and "intrinsic motivation" refers to creating goals and incentives that are not explicitly given to an agent.

My research shows how both domains benefit from abandoning reward-maximization in the underlying objective function.

Thesis
Reward is not Necessary: Foundations for Compositional Non-Stationary Non-Markovian Hierarchical Planning and Intrinsically Motivated Autonomous Agents

My PhD dissertation presents a comprehensive framework, showing how abandoning reward-maximization in the underlying objective function is advantageous for both high-dimensional planning and intrinsic motivation domains. This theory makes it possible to create agents that solve challenging multi-goal tasks in high-dimensions, and propose and evaluate goals deep into the future.

Key Papers
A Unified Theory of Compositionality, Modularity, and Interpretability in Markov Decision Processes

In this theory, we directly optimize predictive maps of events that will occur if an agent acts into the future. By directly optimizing predictive maps instead of value functions, one can compose individual solutions into a composite solution for a long-horizon task with many dynamic variables, such as hunger or hydration states.

Goal Kernel Planning: Linearly-Solvable Non-Markovian Policies for Logical Tasks with Goal-Conditioned Options

In this paper, we decompose long-horizon Boolean logic tasks with the Options Framework, in a way that allows you to solve for an optimal meta-policy as a linear equation. This builds on Emo Todorov's work on Linearly Solvable MDPs, where we extend it to hierarchical state-spaces.

Current Work

I also develop algorithms for Safe AI applications and am currently participating in the MATS program. There has been a recent call to create algorithms that can probabilistically verify an agent's policy satisfies task specifications and safety constraints. My work enables formal verification of policies in high dimensions and creates agents whose underlying decision theory can be modified at test-time. This allows agents to use various decision theories relevant to Safe AI, rather than being limited to expected value theory.

About

I studied genetics in undergrad, but eventually decided to understand more abstract principles of biological intelligence, so I became a computer scientist and started working on Markov Decision Processes. I want to explain where the flexibility of animal intelligence comes from and what principles promote it. My goal is to create the algorithmic foundations for a theory of planning that achieves human-level sample efficiency in complex environments like Atari games.
